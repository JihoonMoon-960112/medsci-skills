## Scope
Advances in Health Sciences Education publishes research on education and training across all health professions, including medicine, nursing, pharmacy, dentistry, and allied health. The journal emphasizes theory-driven research on learning, assessment, clinical reasoning, professional identity formation, and educational interventions, with a strong focus on methodological rigor and theoretical grounding.

## Special Notes
Advances in Health Sciences Education is highly regarded for theory-rich educational research. Unlike Academic Medicine, which leans toward institutional and policy-level studies, this journal prioritizes individual learning processes and educational psychology. Papers must demonstrate clear theoretical framing (e.g., self-determination theory, cognitive load theory, deliberate practice). The journal has a strong international readership and values non-US perspectives.
